About This Project

Seattle Housing Affordability Dashboard · GEOG 458 · Group 8

Project Overview

This dashboard explores housing affordability in Seattle through the lens of rent burden — the percentage of renter households spending more than 30% or 50% of their income on housing. Using data from Seattle City GIS and the U.S. Census, we visualize how rent burden has changed from 2006 to 2022, who is most affected by race and age, and how Seattle's Mandatory Housing Affordability (MHA) zoning policy overlays spatially with these pressures.

This project is an exploratory tool for awareness and advocacy. It is not intended for real estate investment decisions. By focusing on who bears the burden of housing costs, we hope to support housing rights advocates, urban planners, researchers, and residents in understanding Seattle's affordability landscape.

Project Goal

Our primary goal is to make Seattle's housing affordability crisis legible through data visualization — to show not just that rents are high, but who is most burdened, where policy has been applied, and how the situation has evolved over time. We pay particular attention to racial disparities in rent burden, which are often invisible in aggregate statistics.
