Project Overview

Understanding Housing Affordability in Seattle

Seattle is one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States, and rising housing costs have pushed many residents to spend a disproportionate share of their income on rent. This dashboard explores where those burdens are most concentrated, who is most affected, and how local zoning policy relates to affordability across the city.

The primary metrics visualized are median apartment rent by census tract (2001–2025) and rent burden — the percentage of renter households spending more than 35% of their income on housing. An overlay of Mandatory Housing Affordability (MHA) zoning areas provides policy context.

Project Goals

What We Set Out to Show

This project is not a policy analysis. It is an exploratory tool designed to make spatial patterns in housing cost visible in a way that tables and reports cannot. Some tracts have high rents but low burden because incomes are also high. Others are burdened despite relatively modest rents because the people who live there earn very little. Mapping both variables together reveals these nuances.

The Mandatory Housing Affordability overlay allows users to explore how zoning policy is distributed across the city relative to where affordability pressures are greatest. The intended audience includes Seattle renters, housing advocates, urban planners, community organizations, and researchers studying housing inequality.

A note on potential negative impacts: Visualizing areas with high rent burden and low income could contribute to stigmatization or inform real estate speculation. We have mitigated this by focusing on aggregate tract-level trends rather than individual addresses, using neutral language throughout, and making clear this tool is intended for advocacy and awareness — not investment decisions.

Data Sources

Where the Data Comes From

All datasets are publicly available. The three source datasets were processed and merged into a single GeoJSON file at the census tract level using Python. See the assets/readme.md file in the repository for data cleaning documentation.

Dataset Description Source
Apartment Market Rent Prices by Census Tract Median rent per unit and per sq ft by tract, 2001–2025. Includes cost category and year-over-year change classification. Seattle GeoData / CoStar Group
Selected Housing Characteristics (DP04) ACS 5-Year Estimates 2023. Includes GRAPI rent burden breakdown, median gross rent, renter/owner unit counts. U.S. Census Bureau via Seattle GeoData
2020 Census Tracts — Seattle Census tract polygon boundaries for the City of Seattle. Used as the spatial base for all choropleth layers. Seattle GeoData
Mandatory Housing Affordability (MHA) Zones Polygon boundaries of MHA zoning areas with zone type (M, M1, M2) classifications. Seattle GeoData
